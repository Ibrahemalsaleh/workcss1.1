Amblyopia (Lazy Eye) is a condition that affects the vision in one eye. It develops when there's a breakdown in how the brain and the eye work together, and the brain starts to favor one eye over the other. This causes the vision in the weaker eye to deteriorate. Amblyopia is the most common cause of decreased vision in a single eye among children. Unless it is successfully treated in early childhood, vision loss usually persists into adulthood.
